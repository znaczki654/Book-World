5,0 von 5 Sternensehr gut - very good
Vonwildkrautam 9. Februar 2015
Format: Kindle Edition|Verifizierter Kauf
ive read a few other books on being optimistic & all that stuff with various claims which don't deal with the issues at a fundamental enough level but instead end up mostly being various tips to stop being negative without properly saying how .

this book imo manages to explain depression & the mental, emotional & partially also sensual consequences of it . it is well written & explains thought-mistakes & their consequences well . mr burns uses exemplary conversations with patients in order to illustrate the negative thought mistakes & how these support the depressive state (incl. all its possible symthoms like tiredness, lack of concentration which for a very long time i hadn't connected to depression at all, bad memory ..).

negative thinking habits are very widespread in our societies, so it is actually also a book on litterally learning to "feel good", even when you are not already in that mental-emotional state of depression . i can only say that it is well written, is yellow, ive recommended it to close friends & i wish i had discovered this like 15 years ago

ADD :: i still think this book is very good ; for completeness sake, i think it lacks dealing with relaxation & visualisation etc techniques on their own. autogenic training & progressive muscle relaxation have helped me a lot with anxiety & at times just as a way of reducing a base tenseness in daily life. Burns does describe a way of "visualising success", as part of the motivation techniques, that are very similiar in their technique & results.

something the book doesn't deal with, but i don't see as essential, are all the social & behavioural symthoms & results of depression, i.e. "coping" mechanism alcohol, isolating behaviour, people that aren't supportive or destructively enabling, .. ( o'connor deals with these topics in "undoing depression" seen in the mostly emotional context, which i personally find lacking, as i believe that emotions are the results of input and its interpretation through thinking & association with past experiences).

imo the aim of this more straight-forward book is to rediscover the mental & emotional autonomy & resilience that will result in the ability to change those habits & recognise bad influences & to just think clearly again.

some people complain that the book deals with the topic of medication in the last 1-2 chapters, which especially in the u.s. is very everyday ; medication shouldn't be necessary, but when people have lost all hope & faith in their abilities, then pills can be essential as a support, but of course still won't resolve the underlying negative base assumptions that make up the depression & it's results.

additionlly to more focus on relaxation i find lacking, that there isn't any mention of what makes up a healthy balanced diet or at least what (e.g. big amounts of sugar) is unsupportive of a healthy body, which will then make it easier on the mind etc.
